State of Connecticut Statutory Disclosure
Abusive Home Loan Lending Practices Act (Conn. Gen. Stat. § 36a-746)
IMPORTANT PROTECTIONS REGARDING HIGH-COST MORTGAGE TRANSACTIONS

Pursuant to Connecticut regulatory statutes protecting residential mortgage consumers, specific constraints apply to high-cost home loan transactions to mitigate predatory asset terms. Please take notice of the following protections:

  • Restrictions on Prepayment Penalties: No high-cost home loan framework executed in the State of Connecticut shall include prepayment penalty clauses unless explicitly limited within statutory terms and matching designated safe-harbor parameters.
  • No Balloon Payments: High-cost home transactions with an execution lifespan under seven years are prohibited from specifying backend balloon payments or accelerated payment schedules.
  • Mandatory Counseling: Lenders and brokers are strictly prohibited from finalizing a high-cost mortgage connection unless the applicant provides physical certification of completion from an approved HUD/State financial management counselor.
